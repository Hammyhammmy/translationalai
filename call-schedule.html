The Problem

On-call scheduling is a constrained optimization problem disguised as a spreadsheet task. Vacation dates, long weekends, minimum coverage, seniority, fairness across months of shifts—the number of competing constraints exceeds what a person can hold in their head. The result is hours of manual work that still produces schedules someone feels are unfair.

Call Schedule Maker takes the constraints as inputs and produces schedules that are balanced by construction. The team lead stays in control—every assignment can be reviewed, overridden, and locked—but the optimization is handled by the system.

Four Scheduling Modes

Weekly Call Schedule

Fair weekly on-call rotation for teams of 2–15 physicians. Input vacation dates, long weekends, and forced assignments. The system balances shift counts, weekend distribution, and spacing between calls.

Daily Schedule

Day-by-day rotation over weeks to months. Generates multiple pattern variants and scores each by spacing fairness. Choose the pattern that fits your team's preferences.

Academic Rotation Scheduler

Assign residents to clinical rotations (ICU, clinic, elective, teaching) with per-resident requirements and max-consecutive limits. The system runs multiple attempts and returns the best solution.

OR Block Allocator

Assign operating room time across surgeons with anchor slots, day preferences, seniority rules, research commitments, and equipment constraints. Multiple priority policies from equal distribution to subspecialty-driven allocation.

Built-In Fairness

Fairness isn't a report you run after the schedule is made—it's a constraint the algorithm optimizes for during generation. The system tracks shift debt (who's worked more, who's owed rest), weekend and holiday distribution, and spacing between shifts. A fairness dashboard shows the numbers so the team lead can defend the schedule with data, not judgment calls.

Leave Adjudication

Before approving time off, test whether coverage minimums hold on every affected day. The system returns approved or denied with per-day coverage details—no guesswork.

Constraint Editing

Vacation dates, forced assignments, and long weekends are entered in a simple grid editor. Paste from email, edit inline, save versions. Prior schedules are versioned so you can compare and reuse.
