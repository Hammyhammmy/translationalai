The Problem with EMR + AI

Most EMRs were built a decade ago. AI gets added as a layer on top—a plugin, an integration, a third-party widget that reads from the chart but doesn't understand the workflow. The result is two systems: the EMR that runs the clinic, and the AI that tries to help but can't act on what it knows.

LightEMR is built differently. AI isn't a feature—it's part of how the system processes intake, generates documentation, suggests billing, and turns signed notes into orders. The clinic workflow and the AI share the same data, the same context, and the same actions.

Capabilities

Patient Self-Booking

Patients book through your clinic's portal—no app, no account. New patients register in the same flow. The clinic reviews every request before confirmation.

Intake Automation

Faxes, emails, voicemails, and messages are automatically processed, classified, and queued with structured data extracted. Staff acts on intake items, not raw documents.

Charting and Encounters

Full encounter documentation with chart history, medications, labs, imaging, and referrals. Multi-screen support keeps the day sheet and chart in sync across monitors.

Note-to-Actions

When a clinician signs a note, the system extracts actionable items—prescriptions, lab orders, referrals, follow-ups, billing. Staff reviews and creates them in one step.

Scheduling

Provider-managed availability with appointment booking, calendar management, and program-based scheduling. Staff and patients book against the same real-time availability.

Billing

AI reviews signed notes and suggests billing codes based on visit content, with provincial rules enforced. Staff reviews and submits—nothing is auto-billed.

Document Triage

Incoming documents are classified by type and presented with the right actions ready. A prescription renewal shows the extracted medication and a renew button. A lab result shows a file-to-chart button.

Requisition Autofill

Your clinic's lab and imaging forms fill automatically from patient data when you create an order. Ready to send without manual transcription.

Why It Matters

Legacy EMRs treat AI as an add-on. The AI can read the chart, but it can't act in the workflow—it generates suggestions that staff has to manually execute in a separate system. Every handoff between the AI and the EMR is a place where time is lost and errors are introduced.

LightEMR eliminates that gap. A signed note becomes orders, prescriptions, referrals, and billing suggestions in the same system, reviewed by staff in a single step. Incoming faxes become triaged intake items with one-click actions. Patient booking requests land directly in the schedule queue. The AI and the workflow are the same thing.

Data Isolation and Privacy

Each clinic's data is physically isolated—not filtered by a query parameter, but structurally separated so that one clinic's data is invisible to another at the database level. A bug in the application cannot leak data across clinics because the data doesn't exist in the same namespace.

  • All data stored and processed in Canada
  • Physical data isolation per clinic
  • End-to-end encryption in transit and at rest
  • Role-based access control for clinic staff
  • Aligned with PIPEDA and provincial privacy requirements
  • No data used for AI training or shared with third parties
