Foundations

3G
Three Geometries of Failure
The taxonomy that organizes this whole reference. Layers stack. Conduits flow. Networks branch. Each geometry has its own diagnostic question and its own treatment logic. Start here before any specialty doc.
→

Conduits — The Urinary Tract

Urology as the conduit exemplar. Production → expulsion is a five-station pipeline; every clinical problem is "which station failed, and what does the upstream damage look like?"

UT
The Urinary Tract — A Conduit
The five functional stations from kidney to urethra. Storage / voiding state machine, pathophysiology as station failure, diagnostic algorithm matched to station depth, worked case of acute retention from BPH.
→
IA
The Urinary Tract — Interactive Model
One live model of the whole system on real anatomy: watch urine produced and flow to the exit, toggle the storage/voiding state machine and its sympathetic vs parasympathetic nerves, then drop in any of ~30 conditions and watch the physiology break — back-pressure upstream, silence downstream, in the same picture.
→
CN
Common Consults — ER & Inpatient
Nine consult types in the same structure: lesion station, symptom site, pattern, diagnostic move, treatment, and when to call. Triage matrix by tempo. Explicit rules for when to wake the urologist. Worked POD1 post-op retention.
→
PR
Procedures the Trainee Will Do
Foley placement with the difficult-catheterization decision tree. Suprapubic aspiration. Urine collection. CBI setup. Stoma assessment. Indication, technique, failure modes, escalation rules.
→
MD
Medications & Their Stations
α-blockers, 5-ARIs, antimuscarinics, β3 agonists, PDE5 inhibitors, antibiotics. Each mapped onto the station it acts at and the receptor it touches, so the prescribing rationale survives memorization.
→
OP
The Operations — What Actually Happens
TURP, HoLEP, ureteroscopy, robotic prostatectomy, partial nephrectomy. Each operation mapped to its conduit station, what it removes or reconstructs, the day-by-day post-op course, and the complications that come back on call.
→
FU
Clinic Follow-Up Patterns
PSA in context, bladder cancer surveillance, the incidental kidney mass, recurrent UTI in women vs men. The station-by-station clinic note template that makes the next visit possible.
→

Layers — Planned

The geometry where defects sit at depth and present at the surface. Other specialties that fit: dermatology (epidermis → dermis → subcutaneous), neurosurgery (meninges), thoracic surgery (chest wall planes).

AW
The Abdominal Wall — Hernia Anatomy
The original layer-pattern case study. Skin → fascia → muscle → transversalis → peritoneum. Inguinal, femoral, umbilical, incisional. Localizing the fascial defect by exam alone.
→
SK
Skin & Soft Tissue — A Layered Read
Cellulitis, abscess, necrotizing fasciitis, eschar. Which layer the infection is in determines whether antibiotics will reach it.
→

Networks — Planned

The geometry where one source supplies a fan of dependent territories. Failure of a branch is read by what's missing downstream. Cardiology, neurology, vascular surgery, and parts of pulmonology all fit here.

VT
Vascular Trees — Stroke & Ischemia
Territory reading. MCA vs ACA vs PCA stroke. Coronary territories on the ECG. Peripheral arterial disease by Rutherford class. The deficit defines the branch.
→
PN
Peripheral Nerves — Dermatomes & Myotomes
Radiculopathy as a territory map. Plexus injuries. The motor/sensory distribution tells you the level. The history tells you the mechanism.
→
