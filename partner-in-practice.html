Case study

Advanced prostate cancer — ARPI selection.

The flagship application focuses on the complex landscape of advanced prostate cancer treatment. Novel androgen receptor pathway inhibitors — abiraterone, enzalutamide, apalutamide, darolutamide — have made selection preference-sensitive and highly contextual.

The tool queries three patient domains and translates conditional guidelines into an immediate, usable clinical action plan: Recommended, Use with caution, Not recommended.

Domain 01

Disease stage & prior therapy

Establishes the appropriate evidence base by confirming disease stage and treatment history.

Domain 02

Patient factors

Performance status, chemotherapy fitness, cardiovascular risk, history of seizures or falls, and financial capacity — assessed against ARPI contraindications.

Domain 03

Provider & practical factors

Side-effect monitoring needs and automated assessment of drug-drug interactions across the complex metabolic pathways of ARPIs.

Why deterministic

Reproducibility is the contract.

Unlike a chatbot that may answer differently each time, this framework applies clinical reasoning consistently. The tool is not intended to replace clinical judgment — it serves an educational, supporting role with verifiable structured insights and a transparent path from input to recommendation.

Reduced cognitive load

Structured, evidence-based guidance for high-stakes selection decisions.

Increased efficiency

Guidelines streamlined; drug-drug interaction cross-referencing automated.

Transparent insights

Adherence to standards of care, verifiable and structured — supporting, not replacing, clinician judgment.

Roadmap

Beyond prostate cancer.

Modules in development for other urologic oncology domains — side-effects management in ADT, recurrent UTI management — and beyond urology entirely, e.g. antihypertensive selection in essential hypertension. The authoring framework generalizes; the deterministic output contract does not change.
