What it's for

First-line, radiation-free look at the kidneys and bladder: detects hydronephrosis, stones (with shadowing), masses, and post-void residual. Operator-dependent; poor for mid-ureter.

How to read it
  • Both kidneys: size, cortex, and is the central echo (pelvis) split by black urine = hydronephrosis?
  • Echogenic focus + posterior acoustic shadow = stone
  • Bladder: wall, volume, and post-void residual

One finding, three modalities — how the same problem appears on each.
The sign

Back-pressure dilates the collecting system. US shows a black, ballooned pelvis; CT adds the cause (stone, mass) and level; a MAG3 renogram shows whether it's truly obstructing.

Don't be fooled
  • An extrarenal pelvis or full bladder mimics mild dilation
  • Dilation ≠ obstruction — function (MAG3) settles it
