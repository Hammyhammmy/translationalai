How to read it

Every lesion does the same two things a conduit lesion always does: it makes back-pressure upstream (stations dilate — the kidney balloons into hydronephrosis) and silence downstream (stations dim — nothing is getting through). Hematuria is the exception: nothing is blocked, but the cargo changes color from the bleeding station down. The lesion station itself is where flow first fails — it's neither dilated nor silent. That's the whole diagnostic game: find where flow stops, then trace upstream.
