About TranslationalAI

Clinician-led AI tools built on a single principle:
every fact traces to its source, or it is suppressed.

Mission

Clinical AI has a credibility problem. Tools produce fluent, organized output that is wrong or incomplete in ways the clinician must catch by re-reading—error opacity. TranslationalAI was founded to solve this: we build tools where every output traces to its source, and unverifiable content is structurally suppressed. The goal is not a better model, but a tractable audit.

Founder

Hanmu Yan, MD FRCSC is a practicing Canadian urologist and the founder of TranslationalAI. With a background in surgery and applied AI, he develops tools to solve real problems physicians encounter in clinical practice.

The TranslationalAI suite was created with clinical precision and simplicity, designed to integrate seamlessly into healthcare workflows without requiring EMR integration.

Approach

We optimize for verifiability, not fluency. Most clinical AI builders optimize for convenience and output quality. We optimize for making errors findable. Our development approach:

  • Per-fact source provenance—a structural property of the system, not prompt discipline
  • Suppression by default—if a fact can't be traced to its source, it is not emitted
  • Deterministic systems where identical inputs yield identical outputs
  • Privacy and security built into every component
  • Deployed in real clinical environments, not demo-only

Values

  • Clinical First: Every decision prioritizes clinical workflows and patient safety
  • Canadian Built: Designed and hosted in Canada, with Canadian data residency
  • Verifiability Over Fluency: Errors should be findable, not hidden behind polished prose
  • Privacy by Design: Security and privacy are fundamental, not afterthoughts
  • Physician-Led: Developed by practicing physicians who understand daily clinical realities

Privacy and Compliance

All tools are aligned with PIPEDA privacy principles and provincial privacy requirements, with self-assessed alignment to HIPAA security protocols (not certified). They require no EMR integration and are already in daily use by clinicians.
